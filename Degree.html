Is a Degree Really Necessary in the Modern World?

Hi everyone, this is my first vlog, and I want to talk about something important — what does getting a degree really mean?

Is it truly necessary for everyone? Especially when we can gain the same knowledge from YouTube, Google, and research papers — then why is a formal degree still so important?

My Perspective

I believe a degree is important, but not in every situation.

There are certain fields where a degree is like the first door you must open to even enter — fields like medicine or the judiciary. In these areas, formal education is essential. After that, people gain real experience and practical knowledge to perform their work.

What About Software Engineering?

When it comes to software engineering, things are a bit different. Here, what truly matters is your skills and knowledge.

However, there’s still a problem. Many companies prefer candidates with degrees like B.Tech or M.Tech. Because of this, many skilled people don’t even get the chance to apply, which is really disappointing.

"Don’t show us your degree, show us what you can do."

Big tech companies often say this, but in their job postings, they still mention degree requirements. This creates confusion and limits opportunities for many capable individuals.

The Reality of College Education

In my opinion, colleges don’t always teach what companies actually need. They focus more on completing the syllabus rather than preparing students for real-world challenges.

What Does “Software Engineer” Mean to Me?

I consider myself a software engineer — not by degree, but by my work.

To me, a software engineer is not just a job title. It’s a role where we solve real-world problems. We create solutions that make people’s lives easier — whether it’s helping people communicate from different places, enabling digital payments, or building systems that reduce effort and save time.

We are helping make the world more efficient.

Final Thought

For this, we don’t just need degrees — we need opportunities. We need workplaces where managers understand our skills, not just our certificates.
